abstract: |
  This study provides estimates of US spending on 155 conditions, 38 age and sex groups, and types of care from 1996 through 2013. Personal health care spending increased from $1.2 trillion to $2.1 trillion during this period. Diabetes, ischemic heart disease, and low back and neck pain accounted for the largest increases in spending.
